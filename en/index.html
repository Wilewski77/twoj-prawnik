Areas of help

What we help with

Comprehensive legal assistance for foreigners — from residence documents to representation before offices and courts.

Residence legalization

Temporary and permanent residence permits, residence card, EU long-term resident status, visa extensions.

Work in Poland

Work permits, declarations on entrusting work, change of employer, single permit for residence and work.

Polish citizenship

Applications for granting citizenship, recognition as a Polish citizen, Pole's Card, and citizenship confirmation.

Appeals against decisions

Appeals against decisions of the Voivode and the Office for Foreigners, complaints to administrative courts, reopening proceedings.

Family law

Marriages with foreigners, family reunification, divorces, alimony, parental authority, civil status registration.

Labour law

Disputes with employers, unpaid wages, unlawful dismissals, workplace accidents.

Business in Poland

Registration of sole proprietorships and companies by foreigners, contracts, ongoing legal support for business.

Legal representation

Power of attorney before offices, courts and police, procedural documents, deadline monitoring, criminal and civil cases.

Frequently asked

Questions and answers

Do you serve clients outside Łódź?

Yes. We handle administrative cases throughout Poland, and consultations are also available online and by phone. Documents can be sent electronically or by post.

In which languages can I handle my case?

We serve clients in English, Polish, Ukrainian and German. We handle all communication with the authorities on your behalf in Polish.

How much does legal help cost?

The cost depends on the type and complexity of the case. After the first conversation and document review, we present a specific quote — before you decide to work with us.

How long does it take to get a residence card?

It depends on the voivodeship office and the completeness of the application — usually from a few months to over a year. A well-prepared application and active case monitoring significantly shorten the process.

What should I prepare for the first consultation?

Your passport, current residence documents, correspondence with the office (decisions, summonses) and documents concerning work or family. If anything is missing — we'll help you complete it.
